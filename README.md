## Ultrasonic anemometry

[Servizi Territorio srl](https://www.serviziterritorio.it) distributes and assists for Italy the products of [Metek GmbH](https://metek.de), including the uSonic-3 and former USA-1 ultrasonic anemometers.

As a part of its assistance policy Servizi Territorio srl provides technical and usage advice, and sustains them by participating to current research.

A first and concise presentation of ultrasonic anemometers and their use can be found in the seminal paper [Sozzi, R., Favaron, P., 1996, Sonic anemometry and thermometry: theoretical basis and data-processing software, *Environmental software*, 11, 4, 259-270](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0266983896000469). This reference is quite dated, yet is still valuable as a simple introduction to sonic anemometry.

The "mechanics" of the ultrasonic anemometer is deceptively simple, but in popular presentations it is even oversimplified. A bit deeper understanding can be gained by our technical report [The Ultrasonic Anemometer: a deeper than usual view](https://github.com/patti-favaron/temporary-knowledge-base/blob/main/TR_PF_01_2022%20The%20Ultrasonic%20Anemometer.pdf). Reading it is recommended to both ultrasonic anemometer data end users and maintenance technicians; to the latter is especially useful, giving an awareness of the technical problems an ultrasonic anemometer may give.

In its most frequent use, the three-dimensional ultrasonic anemometer is a turbulence profiler. But in the same time it is also a high-resolution, precision anemometer not vulnerable to windl calms: in this role the ultrasonic anemometer may operate as a replacement of conventional, cup-vane anemometers - with a special value at sites characterized by prevailing weak winds. This aspect is explored in the book [Favaron, P., 2025, *Cup Anemometers and Slow Wind*](https://github.com/patti-favaron/temporary-knowledge-base/blob/main/Cup-Anemometers-and-Slow-Wind.compressed.pdf).
